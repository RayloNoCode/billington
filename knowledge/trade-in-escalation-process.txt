## Trade-In Escalation Process

Source: https://www.notion.so/Trade-In-Escalation-Process-2d15bb4b4a65808097bde70e7688cd80

### Purpose
Defines the process for handling trade-in disputes where a returned device cannot be located after confirmed delivery to the returns centre.

### Ownership
- Customer Success: customer communication, tracking, validation, refund processing
- Risk Ops: fraud risk checks
- Operations Director: final approval for escalated refunds

### Customer Success Pathway (5 steps)
1. **Device Return** — customer uses Raylo-provided returns method and retains proof of postage
2. **Proof of Return Validation** — CS validates proof of postage receipt OR tracking link confirming delivery to Raylo warehouse
3. **Delivery Confirmation & Waiting Period** — mandatory 30-day waiting period after delivery confirmed (allows warehouse intake and device reconciliation)
4. **Pre-Escalation Checks (during 30-day period)**:
   - Confirm this is the customer's FIRST trade-in device (check Forest — all subscriptions)
   - If customer has had a previous trade-in, confirm if successful or previously escalated
   - Risk Ops: confirm delivery address has NOT previously received a Raylo device addressed to a different customer
   - CS requests Risk Ops check via Intercom internal note → Risk Ops Fraud bucket → findings returned to CS
5. **Post-14 Day Review** — CS confirms device cannot be physically located AND trade-in status in Forest remains "Declared"

### Escalation Pathway
1. CS submits refund request for full trade-in value in Support Hub
2. Internal approvals obtained per agreed thresholds; escalations tracked at: https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1yfCM_yE24hVM9OLZcs9s_DrymjEr4jE5aZKCxInWqYI/edit?gid=1383121629
3. CS processes refund via #customer-refunds-complaints; CS Lead/Manager approves if all 5 CS Pathway steps met; Operations Director tagged for visibility
4. If CS Pathway steps are NOT all met but refund is still requested → Operations Director approval required
5. CS communicates outcome and expected timeline to customer; case formally closed

### Billington Refund Pre-Checks (MANDATORY before posting any GOGW or processing refund)
Before posting a GOGW (TypeId 61) or processing any trade-in refund on an agreement, Billington MUST check the Anchor agreement for the following blocking transaction types:

| Check | TypeId(s) | Label |
|---|---|---|
| Asset returned | 30 | Asset returned |
| Non-return fee | 28, 63, 64 | Non-return fee (Card), Non-return fee due, Non-return fee applied |
| Asset purchased | 34 | Asset purchased |
| Write-off | 26, 70 | Write-off, Debt sale write-off |
| Asset lost in transit | 46 | Asset lost in transit |

If ANY of these TypeIds exist on the agreement, Billington MUST NOT post the GOGW. Instead, escalate immediately to Charlotte Platt (@Charlotte Platt) or Hargo with the specific transaction found.

Also check for an existing GOGW (TypeId 61) since the request date — if already posted, do not post again.

### Arrears Check (MANDATORY)
Before posting a GOGW, check TotalArrears on the Anchor agreement:
- If TotalArrears > 0: ASK the requester whether to (a) post the GOGW now to offset the arrears, or (b) wait for the customer to clear their arrears first
- If they choose to wait: set a 3-working-day reminder to recheck whether arrears have been cleared
- If they choose to post now: proceed with posting the GOGW
- If TotalArrears = 0: proceed immediately
