Revised Trade-In Refund — Reference
===================================

What it is
----------
A refund pattern distinct from a standard trade-in cashback GOGW.

Standard trade-in:
  Customer declares device → Ingram assesses → assessment matches the
  declaration → cashback flows on the original declared amount across
  the term.

Revised trade-in (this document):
  1. Customer declares device at amount A (e.g. £25.17/mo × 12 = £302.04).
  2. Ingram assesses and revises the offer down to amount B
     (e.g. £12.58/mo × 12 = £150.96).
  3. Customer DECLINES the revised offer (does not consent to keep the
     trade-in at the lower value).
  4. Despite the declination, Ingram pushes the device to stock anyway.
     The customer cannot recover the device.
  5. To make the customer whole, Raylo refunds the DIFFERENCE
     (A − B) — i.e. the cashback they were promised at declaration
     versus what they would have got at the revised offer they did
     not accept.

How to recognise it
-------------------
Thread-side signals (any/all of these):
  - 'revised offer'
  - 'declined the revised'
  - 'cust declined trade in revised offer'
  - 'pushed to stock' / 'processed anyway' / 'already in stock'
  - Two trade-in £ amounts present (declared + revised/accessed) plus an
    explicit 'difference' line.
  - Phrasings: 'Smart declared' (or 'Customer declared'),
               'Smart accessed' (or 'Accessed' / 'Revised').

Math
----
Refund amount = declared total − revised/accessed total.
Always validate this arithmetic against any stated 'difference' value
in the thread. Mistakes here are common — see the 06/05/2026 thread
where Laura's stated £161.08 differed from the actual £302.04 − £150.96
= £151.08 by £10.

GOGW posting
------------
Post a single TypeId 61 transaction at the difference amount, NOT the
declared total or the revised total alone. Notes:
  'Trade-in revised difference refund — declared £A, accessed £B, difference £C'
This makes the audit trail searchable and distinguishes these from
standard trade-in cashback GOGWs in Anchor.

Required evidence before posting
--------------------------------
1. Customer declination of the revised offer (explicit, in thread).
2. Operational signal that Ingram processed the trade-in regardless
   ('pushed to stock', 'in stock', 'already processed').
3. Arithmetic reconciles: declared − revised = stated difference.
4. Where Denton's block exposes both declared and revised/accessed
   totals, those should also reconcile against the thread numbers.

Refuse to post if:
  - Arithmetic doesn't reconcile (off by more than 1p).
  - Denton's declared/accessed differ from the thread amounts by more
    than 1p (and Denton is the source of truth for trade-in totals).
  - Either declination evidence or pushed-to-stock evidence is missing
    — flag and ask for confirmation rather than guessing.

Closed state
------------
Same as standard trade-in: TypeId 61 (GOGW at the difference) plus
TypeId 149 (refund bank transfer) both posted, with no outstanding
TypeId 62 / TypeId 35 reversals. Closed-state detection in the
'confirm next steps' flow uses the same matching rule — just with the
difference as the amount to compare against.
